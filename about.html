Server Handbook

I. The Mission

UM Game Jam 2026 is a 4-day hybrid game development hackathon organized by Persatuan Komputer (PEKOM). It consists of three (3) days online and one (1) physical day.

Your goal is to craft a playable game from scratch based on a secret theme within a limited time. The official language for communication, pitching, and judging is English.

The event kickoff is on 15 April 2026 at 9:00 PM, with the theme revealed at 9:45 PM. Game development runs from 15 April 10:00 PM to 18 April 11:59 PM (GMT+8). Two optional challenges will be revealed throughout the competition for bonus marks!

II. Eligibility & Party Setup

  • > Who Can Join: All undergraduate students aged 18 and above from any public or private university in Malaysia.
  • > Party Size: Solo adventurers or teams of 1 to 4 members. Cross-university and cross-faculty teams are highly encouraged!
  • > No Respecs: Team configurations cannot be changed after registration is submitted.

III. Development Rules & Assets

  • > Genres: All genres are allowed. Horror games are permitted but must display a content warning. Content must not violate laws or IP rights.
  • > Engines: All game engines (including no-code) are permitted. External hosted platforms (e.g., Roblox) are NOT allowed.
  • > Assets: Pre-made assets are permitted as long as they are legally licensed, properly credited, and disclosed.
  • > Generative AI: Generative AI is allowed, however, any AI-generated assets must be explicitly disclosed.

IV. Submissions & Evaluation

Teams must submit one game via the UM Game Jam 2026 itch.io page as a ZIP file by 11:59 PM on 18 April 2026. Required info includes: game title, description, genre, team info, theme integration details, at least 3 screenshots, and the build itself.

  • Web-based (HTML5/WebGL): The .zip root must contain an index.html file, and 'This file will be played in the browser' must be enabled on itch.io.
  • Standalone Build: Windows or macOS tested builds are accepted.

Pitching Day (19 April at PASUM): Teams have up to 4 minutes to pitch and demonstrate the game, followed by a 3-minute Q&A. Judges may play the game during this time.

Criteria: Gameplay & Mechanics, Graphics & Art Style, Creativity, Sound & Music, Polish & Completeness, and Additional Challenges.
